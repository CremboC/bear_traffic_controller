The Menu Screen

It all starts here. To begin playing the game, click the "Play Demo" button.

The Game GUI

Once you have launched the demo you will be taken to the game GUI. It's from here that you can take control of the virtual planes travelling through our airspace. As an ATCO (Air Traffic Control Officer), it is your responsbility to make sure that each plane gets safely from its entry point to its assigned exit point.

Changing Course

If you wish to change an aircrafts flight plan, then simply click on it, select the waypoint you no longer want it to visit, and drag to the waypoing you do want to visit.

Changing Altitude

To change an aircrafts altitude, click on it once to make it the active aircraft, and then click it again to engage altitude control. At this point, moving the scroll wheel on your mouse will move the plane up or down.

Turning left or right

Plane Info Panel

The panel at the bottom left of the screen is used to display various flight specific information that you may require. To select the active flight and display it's information, simply click on the plane.

Radio Console

Every command the user makes is issued to the plane as a radio command. These are displayed in this console.

Score

During gameplay, your score is displayed in the top left corner of the game screen. This score is calculated based on how long you have been playing, and how many planes you have successfully routed through your airsplace.

Game Over

If any two planes in your aispace crash, then the game will end. It is your job to make sure that this doesn't happen. To exit this screen and return to the main menu, simply press any key.

Main Menu Score

Once you have returned to the main menu your score will be displayed on the right side of the game screen. If you choose to play again, you will continue playing from this score.

The Game GUI

The game GUI is entered immediately after the users clicks on Play Demo and is where all game play happens.

Changing Course

To satisfy the "change course" deliverable requirement, the user is able to alter the flight plan of each aircraft has. This involves substituting already selected waypoints for other waypoints in the airspace and is achieved by selecting a flight by clicking on it, and dragging from a waypoint on the flight plan to a waypoint the flight should visit instead.

Changing Altitude

Although the game utilised 2D graphics, the "change altitude" deliverable requirement is met. The game displays each aircraft's current altitude in a label next to it, and in the Plane Info panel at the bottom left of the screen. An aircrafts altitude it changed by clicking on the plane to select it, then clicking again to enable altitude control mode and using the mouse scroll wheel to instruct the plane to move up and down.

Turning left or right

As well as the ability to change an aircraft's course, the user also has the ability to take manual control of each aircraft, therefore satisfying the "turn left or right" deliverable requirement.
